About

I am a PhD student in the Department of Robotics at Hanyang University ERICA, focused on soft robotics and bio-inspired underwater locomotion. My current work investigates decoupled actuator architectures that enable agile, three-dimensional motion in jellyfish-like soft robots.

I am drawn to systems where mechanical compliance, distributed control, and biological intuition converge — machines that move with the elegance of living things.

Research

  1. R/01

    Decoupled Soft Actuators

    Designing soft actuators whose degrees of freedom can be driven independently, expanding the motion repertoire of compliant mechanisms.

  2. R/02

    Bio-inspired Underwater Locomotion

    Translating the swimming and morphing strategies of jellyfish and other soft-bodied organisms into agile, three-dimensional underwater robots.

  3. R/03

    Compliant Mechanism Design

    Studying how distributed compliance and structural geometry shape the dynamics of soft robots — both at design time and during interaction.
